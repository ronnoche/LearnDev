    <p>
      <ul id="tribute-info">Here's a time line of Jesse Robredo's life:
         <li>1958 - born in Naga, Bicol.</li>
         <li>1970 - finished elementary at the Naga Parochial School and entered high school at the Ateneo de Naga University. </li>
         <li>1985 - finished his Masters in Business Administration at the University of the Philippines Diliman as a scholar and was named the Graduate School and Faculty Organization awardee for scholarly excellence.</li>
         <li>1986 - returned to Naga, where he became program director of the Bicol River Basin Development Program (BRBDP), an agency tasked to undertake integrated area development planning in the three provinces of the Bicol region. While working at the BRBDP, he met fellow Nagueño Leni Gerona, whom he would marry the following year.</li>
         <li>1988 - was elected mayor of Naga at age 29.</li>
         <li>1989 - was awarded Philippines Exemplary Fiscal Management Award by the DILG.</li>
         <li>1990 - was awarded Dangal ng Bayan Award by the Civil Service Commission</li>
         <li>1991 - was awarded The Outstanding Young Men of the Philippines</li>
         <li>1995 - was elected president of the League of Cities of the Philippines and chaired the Metro Naga Development Council.</li>
         <li>1996 - was awarded Ten Outstanding Young Persons of the World</li>
         <li>1996 - was awarded Konrad Adenauer Medal of Excellence</li>
         <li>1999 - Asiaweek Magazine credited Robredo with transforming Naga into one of the "Most Improved" cities in Asia.</li>
         <li>1999 - accepted as an Edward Mason Fellow and graduated with a Master of Public Administration degree from Harvard University's John F. Kennedy School of Governmentclass and was an honor student in the field of mathematics.</li>
         <li>2000 - was awarded Ramon Magsaysay Award for Government Service</li>
         <li>2001 - re-elected as city mayor until June 30, 2010.</li>
         <li>2010 - appointed as secretary of the Department of the Interior and Local Government. Focused on relocating thousands of residents in Manila's slums into housing projects to clear Metro Manila's waterways and make way for flood mitigation projects and other development.</li>
         <li>2012 - sudden death by aircraft accident.</li>
         <li>2012 - given the Presidential Lingkod Bayan Award (posthumous) by the Civil Service Commission</li>
      </ul>
